---
Title: "Taking the hit"
Date: "2026-06-16"
---

I always suspected I would not be able to work an office job for long enough to retire from it. Given my personal arrangement in life, for me it has always been very clear this wasn't sustainable in one way or another either logically, emotionally or physically. I despised though that all reasons could show up at once, which in hindsight makes perfect sense.

Now, what is an office job? Working from home is an office job. Staying still for a duration of time, respecting certain rules for as long as it is understood to be the most beneficial thing to do according to one's interests, is also an office job. Over the course of time meaningful work and good compensation have been the components weighting the most in my equation for defining how bearable an office job is.

In my personal economy I deliberately ignored several things about staying resilient because evidently we all live under certain cultural norms, yet, I feel like the rules I am living by nowadays are as complex as those I over the course of my life perceived to be based on the things I learned from history and those I got exposed to while living.

Despite that there are things I didn't ignore and in response to them, over the years I made the intentional effort to save and acquire assets, mostly real state, with the goal of protecting myself from the hard times I was always expecting to come. I am not a pessimist but I know life can get tough. Cycles are everywhere in nature and it is evidently part of the human life. I could've exposed myself to riskier investments with potential to harness higher returns earlier or else invest in assets with greater liquidity, but in my view life is already too volatile for someone to put hard earned money into things they cannot fully understand and control.

This vision has somewhat paid off. As a self taught contract software developer, born, raised and living in South America, the rise of
LLMs have been overwhelming. From the initial release of ChatGPT to being kicked-out the global market it took me roughly just 12 months after almost 10 years of a somewhat stable and growing career. It's been a year now that I've been living off my savings which I am running out of soon. Before the end of this year I should have no savings despite cutting expenses everywhere I can. The local market is also stressed and it isn't only IT. I am having a hard time finding any kind of job.

Self improvement and acquiring knowledge are also things I've been always invested in, with or without money. Alongside taking care of my body, over the past year I've taken courses on hard to automate skills that interest me and have some demand where I live like arboriculture and boat maintenance. Hands on trades that brings me closer to my community and increase my chances to earn a living and even thrive outside the computer screen. Still I cannot find a job. Overall the economy seems really jammed right now. I have no academic degree but I am a healthy and able 39 y/o male that speak 3 languages fluently and have been through a wide range of experiences in life. I should be able to get some sort job but apparently I can't.

So what is left for me? I do think this AI issue is going to get worse in my region. I am not going to be able to come back to software development the way I got used to and built a career on. Investing in real state and living below my means proved to be the right strategy for my own survival because among all the struggles I am facing right now, rent isn't one, debt isn't one and paying the bills wasn't one for enough time to allow me to process those rapid changes and design new strategies for the future. For now it really seems like there is nothing else I can do besides taking the hit and tread on.
